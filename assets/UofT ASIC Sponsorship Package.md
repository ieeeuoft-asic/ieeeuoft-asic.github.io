# **UofT ASIC Team | Sponsorship Package 2026-2027**

## **1\. Introduction: Hello\!**

**We are the UofT ASIC Team.**  
Welcome to the forefront of undergraduate hardware innovation. The UofT Application Specific Integrated Circuit (ASIC) Team is a dedicated student-run organization at the University of Toronto focused on empowering the next generation of semiconductor and microelectronics engineers.  
Historically, IC design has been locked behind graduate-level studies and closed-door industry tools. We are changing that. By leveraging the open-source silicon community (FOSSi) and hands-on project development, we provide undergraduate students with the technical knowledge, practical experience, and resources necessary to design, verify, and actually tape out their own Integrated Circuits.

## **2\. Why Sponsor Us?**

**Connect with the Future of the Semiconductor Industry**  
As the demand for hardware engineers, EDA (Electronic Design Automation) software developers, and microelectronics specialists skyrockets, the UofT ASIC Team offers direct access to a highly specialized, motivated, and pre-vetted talent pool.  
By sponsoring us, you play a vital role in bridging the gap between theoretical undergraduate coursework and real-world industry applications. You will have the opportunity to showcase your brand, recruit top-tier engineering talent, and directly foster the skills your company needs.  
**Our Reach & Impact:**

* **Cross-Canada Network:** Reach students not just at UofT, but across Canada through our CanMUX initiative.  
* **Specialized Talent:** Access students skilled in Digital/Analog IC Design, FPGA development, Hardware Verification, and EDA tool development.  
* **Open Source Pioneers:** Support students actively contributing back to the FOSSi (Free and Open Source Silicon) foundation.

## **3\. Our Key Initiatives (Where You Come In)**

### **💻 The IC Hackathon (Spring Semester)**

Our flagship 7-day hardware hackathon. In 2026, we saw incredible work accomplished by students who rapidly onboarded into IC design through intense workshops and mentorship.

* **The Opportunity:** Students design and submit a full IC design project.  
* **Sponsor Value:** Host a workshop, mentor students 1-on-1, provide a sponsored prize category (e.g., "Best Use of \[Your Company's Tool\]"), and recruit from the brightest participants.

### **🍁 CanMUX (Canadian Microelectronics Undergraduate eXpo)**

The initiative to bring IC design to undergrads is not unique to UofT. We have partnered with schools across the country—including UWaterloo, UCalgary, and York University—to host CanMUX.

* **The Opportunity:** A massive undergraduate-focused IC design conference showcasing incredible student tapeouts and hardware projects.  
* **Sponsor Value:** Unprecedented national exposure. Set up networking booths, deliver keynote speeches, and connect with hardware students from across Canada.

### **🛠️ Design Kompetition & Project Tapeouts**

A year-round, rolling-basis initiative where students pitch, design, and develop their own IC projects. Our goal is to push these designs all the way to a physical tapeout (a 6-month development cycle).

* **The Opportunity:** Students experience the full design cycle: design, layout, optimization, verification, and validation.  
* **Sponsor Value:** Direct mentorship opportunities. Sponsor a specific project's tapeout costs and work closely with a dedicated team of brilliant developers.

### **📚 Education Series & Guest Lectures**

We run a comprehensive asynchronous education series introducing students to open-source IC tools, alongside regular guest lectures from industry professionals.

* **Sponsor Value:** Send your engineers to host a guest lecture. Share your company's real-world challenges, highlight your cutting-edge technologies, and build brand awareness directly in the classroom.

## **4\. Sponsorship Pathways**

We understand that every company has different goals. We offer flexible ways to support the UofT ASIC Team:

* **Monetary Sponsorship:** Direct funding to help us cover event logistics, hackathon prizes, and expensive tapeout costs.  
* **Product & Software (In-Kind):** Access to enterprise-grade EDA tools, hardware development boards (FPGAs), or computing resources.  
* **Mentorship & Judging:** Send your engineers to judge our IC Hackathon, mentor our Design Kompetition teams, or host a technical guest lecture.
